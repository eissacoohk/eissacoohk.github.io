Nokia

Brand Revival Strategy

ABOUT

Nokia, (est. 1865) is one of the most established and renowned international brands. As an iconic and innovative brand, it has produced some of the world’s most loved mobile phones and is considered to be pioneers in the mobile and telecommunications industry.

THE PROJECT

This project was a self-initiated and non-commissioned collaborative project.
Nokia was once the world’s leading mobile phone producers, but since the introduction of the iPhone by Apple in 2007, it has struggle and been unable to re-establish itself as the iconic and innovative mobile phone brand it once was. The project was to revive the once iconic brand, devising a brand strategy to reintroduce the brand back into the market.

THE OUTCOME

Through research into the reasons for Nokia’s demise, it was established that Nokia had little negative brand equity, it was simply forgotten. The solution was therefore to reposition the brand for entry into new markets in the developing world, where there is a proven opportunity for growth.
Africa was chosen for its potential and huge market opportunity, and acts as the entry point into the developing world for this brand revival. Branding was developed to help position Nokia as an iconic, international brand to the intended target market of aspirational young consumers – which accounts for the majority of Africa’s growing population.
