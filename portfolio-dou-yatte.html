Dou Yatte

A guide to basic cooking skills

THE PROJECT

This was a self-initiated project showcased at the Set in Motion AUT Communication Design Graduate Exhibition 2016.
’Dou Yatte’ roughly translates to ’How do I do this?’ in Japanese, and is a project that explores the application of different Japanese ideologies – mu (emptiness), ma (space) and kawaii (cute), to procedural instructional depictions of how to execute basic cooking skills.

THE OUTCOME

The outcome was a set of three books, each reflecting the same set of instructional depictions of basic cooking skills such as how to dice an onion or crack an egg. Each book however, reflects a slightly different interpretation that is aligned with the designer’s comprehension of the associated Japanese ideology.
Kawaii (cute) reflects the cute aesthetic and playful tone of the Japanese cultural phenomenon in Japan.
Ma (space) plays on the concept depicted in Hayao Miyazaki’s animated films, where a gap, space or pause is used to build tension between two structural parts.
Mu (emptiness) reflects the ideology as described by Kenya Hara, where emptiness acts as a vessel that allows the user to add their own meaning and interpretations.
