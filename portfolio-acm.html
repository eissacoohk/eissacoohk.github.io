Auckland City Mission

Visual Brand Identity Refresh

ABOUT

Auckland City Mission is non-government, national organisation providing unique and specialised health and social services for marginalised Aucklanders. It was founded in 1920 as The Mission by Reverend Jasper Calder, who wanted to extend the Anglican church’s services beyond spritual guidance and hope to physically and socially aid those suffering from poverty, sickness or old age.

THE PROJECT

This project was a self-initiated and non-commissioned collaborative project.
Many non-for-profit or non-government organisations lack the time and resources to refresh their visual brand identity because they invest it all towards their cause – to help others. The project was to refresh to visual brand identity of a non-for-profit organisation and create a stronger, more cohesive brand for the organisation.

THE OUTCOME

The new visual identity and brand for Auckland City Mission better reflects the happiness, stability and trust the organisation offers its clients. The overall brand was pulled together using a consistent cohesive visual identity system reflected in a thorough brand guideline.
The new logo stays true to the hand-drawn style and key elements of the original. Keeping the discrete Catholic cross hidden between the typography and illustration, the new logo was cleaned up with a custom-designed typeface and cleaner illustrations, helping to align the brand as a friendly and trustworthy organisation.
