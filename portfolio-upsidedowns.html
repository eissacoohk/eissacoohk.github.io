UpsideDowns

Brand Extensions and Touchpoints

ABOUT

UpsideDowns is a non-for-profit organisation that works to support the education of children with Down syndrome in New Zealand. They raise funds to help subsidise the expensive costs for speech therapy â€“ a vital stage to ensuring children with Down syndrome are best prepared to communicate and are able to interact with others and live to their potential.

THE PROJECT

This was a self-initiated and non-commissioned collaborative project. The initial branding identity refresh was undertaken by Anna Massey and Nathan Clark.
Non-for-profits often have limited budget and resources to develop a brand that extends itself across brand extensions and touchpoint which will enable the organisation to have strong fundraising and marketing strategies that will attract corporate sponsors. The project was to develop a strategy using brand extensions and touchpoints that would attract a strong base of donors and corporate sponsors.

THE OUTCOME

A wide range of touchpoints and brand extensions were developed to not only attract corporate sponsors but create a stronger and more supportive Down syndrome community. This included strategies like an optometrist partnership, an online community board and a library bus with specially selected books for children with Down syndrome. The application and user journey for these brand extensions and touchpoint was also considered and analysed.
A call-to-action video was also created to convey the message that a child with Down syndrome can live to his or her full potential with the right support and help, asking corporate sponsors to get on board and support a child with Down syndrome through UpsideDowns.
